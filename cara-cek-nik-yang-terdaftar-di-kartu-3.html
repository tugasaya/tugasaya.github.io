This is none other than to protect customers and the public from frauds that are commonly carried out via SMS or telephone and other crimes, minimize misuse of numbers, and lead to a single identity number.

It's no joke that the Ministry of Communication and Information has enforced this, the Director General of Post and Information Technology (PPI) of the Ministry of Communication and Information Technology (Kemkominfo), Achmad M. Ramli, said that the government will impose sanctions on cellular service users if they do not register prepaid cards according to official identity.

If you do not immediately re-register a prepaid card, the initial sanction that will be imposed on the customer is that the customer cannot make outgoing calls. If it is still being continued, the next penalty is that customers will not be able to receive calls. The heaviest penalty imposed is that numbers will be blocked.

"Each is a maximum of 30 days. But 15 days if you have received a second warning in the form of an unreachable number it will be blocked afterwards," he said.

Re-registration of cellular cards is enforced since entering the second week of October. For those of you who have not re-registered your cellular card, you should do so immediately so you won't be penalized and can still continue to use your mobile card.
