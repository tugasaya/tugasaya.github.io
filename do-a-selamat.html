Simple Present Tense

Digunakan utk menyatakan Kegiatan yg dilakukan berulang, rutin, kebiasaan (habitual) dan kebenaran umum (fakta).

Ket. waktu Present Tense

always

often

Rumus Present Tense

(+) S + Verb 1 (-s/es) + O/C

(-) S + Do/Does + not + Verb 1 + O/C

(?) Do/Does + S + Verb 1 + O/C?

=> Subject + Auxiliary

I, you, we, they + do

He, She, it + does

26. Government is the most responsible if Capital Flight happens in a country. Do you agree or disagree to the statement? Selamat malam guru2 yang baik hati, saya minta tolong untuk jawabanya minta tolong di buatkan 4 paragraph. terima kasih


Government is the most responsible if Capital Flight happens in a country.

The consequences of capital flight vary depending on the level and type of government reliance on foreign capital. Capital flight had a more severe effect during the Asian crisis of 1997. During the crisis, the Asian tigers' rapid currency devaluations triggered a capital flight, resulting in a domino effect of falling stock prices around the world.

According to some reports, the crisis caused a 60 percent drop in worldwide markets. The International Monetary Fund (IME) stepped in and provided bridge loans to the afflicted economies. The countries also bought US treasuries to help their economies. In comparison to the Asian financial crisis, the stated effect of a 2015 devaluation of the Chinese yuan that resulted in capital outflows was comparatively moderate, with the Shanghai stock market declining by only 8%.

To deal with the consequences of capital flight, governments use a variety of tactics. They impose capital controls, for example, to limit the flow of their money outside the country. However, this may not always be the best option, as it may further lower the economy and increase public anxiety about the situation. Furthermore, the advancement of international technology advances, such as bitcoin, may aid in the circumvention of such controls.

The negotiation of tax treaties with other jurisdictions is another popular strategy utilized by governments. One of the key reasons why capital flight is appealing is that there are no tax penalties associated with shifting funds. Countries can take away some of the benefits derived from such transactions by making large currency transfers across borders more expensive.
